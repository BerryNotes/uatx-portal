Unpaid Internship Support

◀ Back to Student Portal

UATX will offer an Educational Support award of up to $2,000 to students who secure unpaid summer internships. The purpose of the award is to help offset general living expenses that may otherwise limit access to learning opportunities aligned with a student's academic and career goals.

This award is not compensation for services and does not create an employment relationship with UATX.

Eligibility

  • Applicants must be a rising junior or senior (in the summer award is utilized) in good academic standing, currently enrolled at UATX, with the intention to return in the fall.
  • Applicants must have secured (or received an offer for) an unpaid, career-focused summer internship.
  • Internships are expected to involve a meaningful time commitment appropriate to the nature of the opportunity.
  • Award amounts may vary based on duration, financial need, and available funding.

Deadline

Applications should be submitted to Nick Vainisi at nvainisi@uaustin.org and will be reviewed on a rolling basis through May 1, 2026.

Decisions will be made within a reasonable time frame following submission.

Application Process

Students must submit a one-page summary statement that includes:

  • An overview of the student's academic program, internship details, and how the experience will support their educational and career goals
  • Internship organization name, job description, and the name and contact information of the supervisor
  • A summary of the intended use of the award funds

Post-Internship Requirement

Following completion of the internship, award recipients must submit a summary describing:

  • The internship experience
  • How the opportunity contributed to their academic or career development

Allowable Use of Funds

Funds may be used at the student's discretion to help offset general expenses incurred during the internship period, including but not limited to:

  • Housing
  • Food
  • Transportation
  • Relocation or travel expenses

Students are responsible for managing the funds and determining their personal budget.

Award Amounts

  • Awards are capped at $2,000 per student
  • Award amounts may be less than the maximum based on:
    • Demonstrated financial need
    • Internship duration
    • Available program funding

Disbursement

  • Awards are disbursed directly to students via ACH through our expense management system, Ramp.com
  • Disbursement may occur as a lump sum or in installments
  • Students must complete all required acceptance and verification steps prior to payment (signed award acceptance, internship start verification, active enrollment verification, must have W9 on file)

Tax Notice

Funds provided through this program may be considered taxable income under IRS regulations. The university does not withhold taxes from these payments. Students are responsible for any tax obligations associated with receipt of the award.

*Award is subject to forfeiture or reimbursement should the student change internship status, quit, or terminate internship opportunity early.

Finance Skills Development

◀ Back to Student Portal

Several students have expressed interest in gaining hands-on finance skills that will make you more competitive for internships and future roles in investment banking, private equity, corporate finance, or financial consulting. UATX is offering a partially funded opportunity for motivated students to complete a self-study finance training program, with reimbursement of up to $250 upon successful completion.

These programs are designed to provide practical knowledge of the finance industry and help you develop the core competencies that employers value, including financial modeling, valuation, and Excel skills. Completing them will not only strengthen your resume but also give you the terminology and confidence needed to communicate effectively in conversations and interviews.

Listed are several self-study programs recognized by many companies in the industry. While completing one of these programs does not guarantee an internship, it will provide you with a stronger knowledge base and demonstrable skills that can support your professional development and career pursuits. Each program offers unique content, so we encourage you to review them and determine which best aligns with your goals. The overview below provides a brief snapshot of each program, but is not comprehensive of all you will learn.

If you choose to complete one of these courses over the holidays or during the Winter or Spring Terms in 2026, we will reimburse up to $250 upon submission of proof of successful completion. They do represent a substantial investment of time and effort, so plan accordingly.

Training the Street

Foundations + Advanced
Cost: $500 (we will reimburse up to $250)

What You Will Learn

  • Excel techniques specifically tailored for financial modeling
  • Time value of money, discounting, and other foundational finance math
  • Understanding and interpreting the income, balance sheet, and cash flow statements
  • Accounting implications when companies merge or get acquired
  • Fundamentals of building integrated 3-statement models from scratch
  • Modeling cash sweeps (i.e., using excess cash to pay down debt)
  • Core valuation techniques, like DCF
  • Valuing companies using peer multiples
  • Valuation based on precedent transactions (M&A deals)
  • The basics of LBOs, including capital structure and return drivers
  • Detailed modeling of cash flows and discount rates
  • Building full LBO models in Excel
  • Building merger models (pro forma statements, synergies, etc.)
  • Understanding sources of capital, debt vs equity, and how corporations fund transactions

Why It's Valuable

These topics cover what analysts and interns actually work on in finance roles. You don't just learn how to model, you also learn how to value, analyze deals, and use Excel.

Wall Street Prep

Financial & Valuation Modeling (Premium Package)
Cost: $499 (we will reimburse up to $250)

What You Will Learn

  • Building 3-statement financial models from scratch
  • Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) valuation
  • Trading and transaction comparables
  • Mergers & Acquisitions (M&A) modeling
  • Leveraged Buyout (LBO) modeling

Why It's Valuable

This program mirrors the real-world modeling tasks that analysts and interns perform on the job. It's hands-on, practical, and highly regarded in the finance industry.

Breaking Into Wall Street

Premium Package
Cost: $497 (we will reimburse up to $250)

What You Will Learn

  • Core financial modeling (3-statement modeling, forecasting)
  • Valuation (DCF, comps, precedent transactions)
  • M&A and LBO modeling
  • Excel/VBA for financial modeling
  • Interview case studies and practical exercises used by banks and consulting firms

Why It's Valuable

Extremely comprehensive and widely used by students preparing for internships. Includes hundreds of hours of video tutorials, exercises, and practice models.

About AEI

The American Enterprise Institute (AEI) is a Washington, D.C.–based public policy think tank that promotes ideas rooted in free markets, limited government, individual liberty, and a strong national defense. It conducts research on economics, foreign and domestic policy, health care, education, and constitutional studies. AEI is known for emphasizing empirical research and debate rather than advocating a single party platform. Its scholars frequently influence public discussion through books, media commentary, and testimony before Congress.

2026 Summer Honors Program

The Summer Honors Program at the American Enterprise Institute (AEI) is an intensive, fully-funded educational and professional development opportunity for top undergraduate students. Situated in the heart of Washington, DC, this program provides participants the opportunity to connect with the ideas, research, and network of AEI–one of America's preeminent policy think tanks. This will occur during an intensive one-week seminar led by an AEI scholar or partner instructor.

The program assembles a diverse cohort of students from a wide range of ideological perspectives to engage in substantive dialogue and debate about the most pressing issues facing the country and world. The core feature of the program is a series of discussion-based seminars that offer participants forums to delve deeply into policy areas of their choice. Outside of the seminars, students participate in briefings with distinguished guests, high-level networking opportunities, and site visits in Washington.

The program aims to provide students with a deeper understanding of contemporary public policy challenges, a greater appreciation for substantive dialogue across ideological divides, and the knowledge and networks to pursue a future career in the policy world.

If Not Now Fellowship

May 17–20, 2026 | New York City

The If Not Now Fellowship is a week-long immersive experience for mid- to upper-level college student leaders that will equip them to stand up for their Jewish peers and confront antisemitism on their campuses.

Details

Participating students will be offered a $500 travel stipend, and hotel and meals are covered during the program.

The curriculum combines lectures on antisemitism (its history, manifestations, how to recognize antisemitic bias on their campuses, etc.), rights the students hold on campus to be able to advocate for their Jewish peers (likely folks from FIRE or other nonprofits that focus on campus free speech), and excursions around NYC that tie into the programmatic themes of the fellowship.

By the end of the fellowship, participants will emerge with:

  • A deeper understanding of how to identify and productively confront antisemitism
  • Practical tools to stand up for their Jewish peers in their communities
  • A supportive network of student leaders and nonprofit professionals
  • The confidence to lead with integrity and purpose

Accommodations will be arranged for Jewish students observing the holiday of Shavuot.

Ideal Candidates

AHA Foundation is accepting 15 students, and hope to include a mix of Christian, Jewish, and non-religious participants.

Admissions are rolling until the 15 student cohort is filled.

Selected students should have a predisposition to be pro-Israel; during the program, AHA works to deepen their understanding of why that position matters and how to engage effectively with peers who hold different views.

Students who are accepted will have shown leadership on their campus whether that be a part of a formal role in campus clubs or informal in the classroom.

How to Apply

Send an email to Nick Vainisi (nvainisi@uaustin.org) with a CV and why you are interested in attending the program.

About the Ronald Reagan Institute

The Ronald Reagan Institute serves to advance the principles and legacy of President Ronald Reagan in today's policy landscape. The Institute convenes bipartisan leaders, produces research on national security and economic prosperity, and develops the next generation of public servants through its fellowship and internship programs. Through initiatives such as the Reagan National Defense Survey and high-level forums, it plays a leading role in shaping conversations around American leadership, democratic values, and global security.

Programs & Fellowships

Academy for Civic Education and Democracy (early June–late July)

In partnership with the George Washington University, ACED offers high-achieving college students the opportunity to spend a summer learning leadership skills and civic engagement while immersed in Washington DC life. Students participate in an internship with one of our DC-based partners and take a class about presidential leadership at RRI. They live in GWU housing, take field trips, and participate in networking opportunities to make their time in DC uniquely meaningful!

Civic Leaders Fellowship (November–April)

Our CLF students participate in a six month leadership and professional development program designed to cultivate them into the next generation of civic-minded professionals. They meet six times — thrice virtually, twice in Washington, DC, and once at the Reagan Library in Simi Valley, CA. They'll be paired with a professional mentor in RRI's network and learn real-world skills and ideas that will translate far beyond their college graduation.

National Defense Fellowship (September–December)

Run in conjunction with the Alexander Hamilton Society, NDF seeks to educate about 20 advanced students in the vocabulary, theory, practice, and organization of U.S. defense policy. The main components of the fellowship include the Peace Through Strength Boot Camp in September, a four day weekend program in Washington DC that introduces the fundamentals of national security policy with leaders in the field, and participation in the Reagan National Defense Forum, where they'll get to hear from Secretaries of Defense, Chairmen of the Joint Chiefs of Staff, members of Congress, industry leaders, and more at a day long conference in Simi Valley, CA.

American Opportunity Bootcamp (October)

AOB is a three day weekend convening in Washington DC that seeks to educate undergraduates about economic policymaking process and explore current ideas and debates in applied political economy — including the role of research, business, interest groups, lobbying, think tanks, and journalism in the policymaking process; and current policy debates related to economics and opportunity.

Study Abroad in Israel

June 21 – July 1, 2026 | Israel

We are pleased to invite applications for an 11-day study abroad program in Israel. Supported by a generous grant from the Dell Foundation, this program offers an opportunity to explore the history, culture, and geopolitics of the region. The trip will entail a 1.5 credit hour course taught by Prof. JJ Kimche on Israel's past and future, and will require a 3–5 hour Zoom seminar in the week prior to the program.

The trip, organized by Herut, immerses students in the complexities of Israeli sovereignty, security doctrine, and history through strategic visits to Jerusalem, Judea & Samaria, and the Gaza Envelope. Participants will engage in high-level meetings with Knesset members and military strategists, alongside touring iconic historical sites such as the City of David and Masada to understand the continuity of Jewish history. The itinerary concludes in Tel Aviv with a focus on the future, balancing the nation's founding story with its modern innovation economy and global influence.

Key Details

  • Dates: June 21 – July 1
  • Subsidized Cost: $2,000 (includes round-trip airfare, lodging, and programming). Due by May 1, 2026.
  • Eligibility: Open to students with an academic average of 83 or higher. If you do not have CSA yet or are near the threshold, please apply.

How to Apply

Space is strictly limited. Participants will be selected based on academic standing and a statement of interest.

Deadline: Applications are due by Friday, January 30 at the start of the day (8:00 AM CST).

About the Texas Stock Exchange

The Texas Stock Exchange (TXSE) is a newly established national securities exchange headquartered in Dallas. Founded with the goal of creating a more competitive, issuer-friendly alternative to legacy exchanges, TXSE seeks to streamline listing requirements, reduce regulatory friction, and attract companies and investors who value transparency, innovation, and choice. Positioned at the intersection of Texas's fast-growing economic ecosystem and the broader U.S. capital markets, TXSE aims to become a major trading venue that supports entrepreneurial growth, investment, and long-term economic vitality.

How to Apply

Deadline: February 20, 2026

  • Are you currently following a specific market, index, asset class, or sector? If so:
    • Which one are you following?
    • Why did you choose it?
    • What have you been learning from tracking it?

Students will be chosen based on clarity of interest, thoughtfulness of responses, demonstrated curiosity about markets, and overall professionalism.
